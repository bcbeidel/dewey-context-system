# Systematic Review: Best Practices

**Purpose**: Guidelines for conducting high-quality systematic literature reviews.

---

## Paper Selection

- **Quality over quantity**: 10 high-quality papers > 30 mediocre ones
- **Include foundational works**: Seminal papers even if old
- **Recent developments**: At least 30% from last 3 years
- **Methodological diversity**: Mix RCTs, meta-analyses, qualitative studies

---

## Critical Reading

- Don't accept findings at face value
- Check sample sizes and effect sizes
- Note conflicts of interest
- Consider alternative explanations
- Assess methodological rigor

---

## Citation Practices

- Always include year: (Author, Year)
- Use "et al." for 3+ authors: (Smith et al., 2020)
- Direct quotes need page numbers: (p. 42)
- Paraphrasing still requires citation
- Maintain consistent citation style throughout

---

## Synthesis Quality

- Organize by theme, not by paper
- Identify patterns and contradictions
- Note effect sizes when available
- Distinguish correlation from causation
- Connect findings to theoretical frameworks

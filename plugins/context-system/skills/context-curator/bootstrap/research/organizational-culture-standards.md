# Organizational Culture & Ethnographic Research Standards

**Purpose**: Best practices for organizational culture assessment and focused ethnography

**Created**: 2026-02-08
**Status**: Research synthesis for `/researcher` skill implementation

---

## Overview

Organizational culture research encompasses methods from structured assessments (1-2 weeks) to focused ethnography (4-8 weeks) for understanding culture, work practices, and organizational dynamics.

**Core principle**: Match depth of study to research questions and available time - not all culture research requires months-long ethnography.

---

## Three Approaches (Lightest to Deepest)

### 1. Structured Culture Assessment (1-2 weeks)

**Tools**: OCAI, Denison Model
**Best for**: Organizational culture assessment for business decisions
**Output**: Quantitative culture profile + actionable insights

### 2. Applied Organizational Research (2-4 weeks)

**Methods**: Surveys + interviews + observation
**Best for**: Tool adoption, change readiness, data culture barriers
**Output**: Mixed methods understanding of organizational dynamics

### 3. Focused Ethnography (4-8 weeks)

**Method**: Deep immersion and observation
**Best for**: Cultural understanding, work practice patterns
**Output**: Rich cultural description and theory

## Part 1: Structured Culture Assessment

### OCAI (Organizational Culture Assessment Instrument)

**Framework**: Competing Values Framework (CVF)

**Four culture types**:
1. **Clan Culture**: Collaborative, family-like, focus on mentoring
2. **Adhocracy Culture**: Innovative, dynamic, risk-taking
3. **Market Culture**: Results-oriented, competitive, goal-driven
4. **Hierarchy Culture**: Structured, controlled, process-oriented

**Source**: [About OCAI](https://www.ocai-online.com/about-the-Organizational-Culture-Assessment-Instrument-OCAI)

---

### OCAI Six Dimensions

Assessment across six key dimensions:
1. **Dominant characteristics**: What defines the organization?
2. **Organizational leadership**: Leadership style and approach
3. **Management of employees**: How people are treated and managed
4. **Organizational glue**: What holds organization together
5. **Strategic emphases**: What the organization emphasizes
6. **Criteria of success**: How success is defined and rewarded

**Process**: Rate current state and preferred future state for each dimension.

**Output**: Visual profile showing culture type distribution.

**Source**: [OCAI Framework](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41599-025-05819-3)

---

### Denison Organizational Culture Model

**Orientation**: Culture as it relates to financial performance.

**Four traits** (12 indexes):
1. **Mission**: Strategic direction, goals, vision
2. **Adaptability**: Change orientation, customer focus, organizational learning
3. **Involvement**: Empowerment, team orientation, capability development
4. **Consistency**: Coordination, agreement, core values

**Strength**: Links culture to business outcomes (revenue growth, profitability, market share).

**Source**: [Denison Organizational Culture Survey](https://denisonconsulting.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/08/introduction-to-the-denison-model.pdf)

---

### OCAI vs. Denison Comparison

| Aspect | OCAI | Denison |
|--------|------|---------|
| **Focus** | Organizational effectiveness | Financial performance |
| **Framework** | Competing Values Framework | Mission/Adaptability/Involvement/Consistency |
| **Use case** | Culture type identification | Performance-linked culture assessment |
| **Timeline** | 1-2 weeks | 1-2 weeks |

**Both**: Based on Competing Values Framework, validated and reliable.

**Source**: [Culture Assessment Commonalities - LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/illustration-denison-model-ocai-culture-factor-russ-davis)

---

### Implementation Best Practices

**Data collection**:
1. Survey all or representative sample of organization
2. Collect demographic data for segment analysis
3. Conduct follow-up interviews with key stakeholders

**Analysis**:
- Identify current vs. preferred culture gaps
- Analyze by department, level, tenure
- Identify patterns and outliers

**Synthesis**:
- Develop clear, actionable strategies
- Make strategies specific, measurable, aligned with goals
- Prioritize high-impact, feasible changes

**Timeline**: 1-2 weeks (survey design, distribution, analysis, reporting)

**Source**: [Understanding Cultural Assessment Tools](https://www.jointhecollective.com/article/understanding-cultural-assessment-tools-a-comprehensive-guide-for-emerging-leaders/)

---

### Validity and Reliability

**Evidence**: Reliability and validity of CVF model and OCAI scales demonstrated by:
- Denison and Mishra, 1995
- Hartnell et al. 2011
- Howard, 1998
- Lamond, 2003

**Recent concerns**: Original analytical methods (clustering based on ipsative scales) pose methodological limitations. Recent research recommends methodological refinement.

**Source**: [Capturing Blended Subcultures - Nature](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41599-025-05819-3)

## Part 2: Applied Organizational Research

### When to Use

**Appropriate for**:
- Tool adoption research ("Why aren't teams using the dashboard?")
- Change readiness assessment
- Data culture barriers
- Post-implementation reviews

**Timeline**: 2-4 weeks

**Methods mix**: Surveys + interviews + limited observation

---

### Data Collection Methods

**Quantitative** (surveys):
- Culture surveys (OCAI, Denison)
- Tool usage surveys
- Satisfaction and engagement surveys

**Qualitative** (interviews):
- Semi-structured interviews with stakeholders (10-20 people)
- Focus groups with teams (3-5 groups of 6-8 people)
- Document analysis (policies, communications)

**Observation** (limited):
- Meeting observations (3-5 meetings)
- Work shadowing (selected roles, 1-2 days each)
- Tool usage observation

---

### Analysis Approach

**Mixed methods integration**:
1. Analyze quantitative (survey patterns, correlations)
2. Analyze qualitative (thematic coding of interviews)
3. Integrate via joint displays (themes + statistics)
4. Triangulate findings across sources

**Output**: Comprehensive understanding of organizational dynamics with both breadth (survey) and depth (interviews).

## Part 3: Focused Ethnography

### Definition and Characteristics

**Focused ethnography**: Methodological adaptation for social research in emerging contexts with:
- Researchers with background knowledge
- Specific research questions
- Intensive, short-term data collection (vs. traditional long-term)
- Time-limited settings

**Timeframe**: 4-8 weeks (vs. 6-24 months for traditional ethnography)

**Source**: [Focused Ethnography: Methodological Adaptation](https://www.qualitative-research.net/index.php/fqs/article/view/2182)

---

### 2024-2025 Developments

**Digital Ethnography in Modern Workplaces**:
- Digitalization changes work and research complexity
- Ethnographic fieldwork includes online and offline settings
- Modern workplaces are fluid, hybrid, multi-located

**Multisitedness**: Essential for understanding dispersed work - researchers engage with multiple relevant spaces to understand how informants experience flexible environments.

**Source**: [Digital Ethnography in Modern Workplace (2025)](https://www.emerald.com/insight/content/doi/10.1108/joe-11-2024-0085/full/html)

---

### Walking Ethnography (2025)

**Emerging approach**: Walking ethnography as mode of qualitative experimentation allowing researchers to experience field in embodied ways reflecting fluidity and unpredictability of contemporary organizational spaces.

**Application**: Especially relevant for hybrid/distributed work environments.

**Source**: [Experimentation in Qualitative Organization Research (2025)](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/10944281241229787)

---

### Focused Ethnography Data Collection

**Intensive methods**:
- **Participant observation**: Immersion in organizational setting (4-8 weeks)
- **In-depth interviews**: Repeated interviews with key informants
- **Document analysis**: Organizational artifacts, communications
- **Field notes**: Detailed descriptive notes of observations
- **Audio/video recording**: Meetings, interactions (with permission)

**Reflexivity**: Researcher actively reflects on their influence and perspective.

---

### Analysis Methods

**Ethnographic analysis**:
1. **Immersion**: Read all field notes, transcripts, documents
2. **Coding**: Identify patterns, themes, cultural meanings
3. **Thick description**: Rich, detailed narrative of culture
4. **Interpretation**: Connect patterns to broader cultural understanding
5. **Validation**: Member checking with informants

**Output**: Cultural description and theoretical insights about organizational life.

---

### Social Work Applications (2024)

**Recent exploration**: Focused ethnography for social work researchers examining social justice issues within specific sub-cultures and service systems.

**Note**: FE methods remain underspecified, requiring more methodological guidance.

**Source**: [Focused Ethnography in Social Work Research (2024)](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/14733250231214199)

## Choosing the Right Approach

### Decision Matrix

| Research Question | Approach | Timeline | Output |
|------------------|----------|----------|--------|
| What is our culture type? | OCAI/Denison | 1-2 weeks | Culture profile |
| Why low tool adoption? | Applied Research | 2-4 weeks | Barriers + recommendations |
| How do teams really work? | Focused Ethnography | 4-8 weeks | Cultural description |

---

### Experience Requirements

**Structured Assessment**: Some experience (can use validated instruments)

**Applied Research**: Some to experienced (requires mixed methods skills)

**Focused Ethnography**: Experienced (requires cultural sensitivity, reflexivity, theoretical grounding)

---

## Quality Criteria

### For Structured Assessments

- Valid, reliable instruments (OCAI, Denison)
- Representative sample
- Appropriate analysis methods
- Actionable recommendations

### For Applied Research

- Multiple data sources (triangulation)
- Systematic data collection
- Transparent analysis
- Integration of qual + quant

### For Focused Ethnography

- Extended engagement in field
- Thick description
- Reflexivity documented
- Member checking with participants
- Cultural sensitivity

---

## Timeline Summary

| Approach | Timeline | Phases |
|----------|----------|--------|
| **Structured Assessment** | 1-2 weeks | Design (2 days) → Survey (1 week) → Analysis (2-3 days) → Report (2 days) |
| **Applied Research** | 2-4 weeks | Planning (3 days) → Surveys (1 week) → Interviews (1-2 weeks) → Analysis (1 week) → Report (3 days) |
| **Focused Ethnography** | 4-8 weeks | Entry (1 week) → Immersion (3-6 weeks) → Analysis (1 week) → Writing (1 week) |

---

## When to Use Organizational Culture Research

**✅ Use when**:
- Assessing data culture or readiness
- Understanding tool adoption barriers
- Change management planning
- Post-merger cultural integration
- Organizational transformation

**❌ Skip when**:
- Quick answers needed (<1 week available)
- Culture not relevant to research question
- Cannot access organization sufficiently

---

## Integration with Other Methods

**Common combinations**:
- **Culture Assessment + Case Study**: Culture context for implementation case
- **Applied Research + UX Research**: Organizational factors + user needs
- **Focused Ethnography + Case Study**: Deep cultural understanding in case context

## Overview

UX research encompasses methods to understand user needs, behaviors, and attitudes to inform product design decisions.

**Core principle**: Real users over synthetic users - authentic research with actual target users is essential.

---

## Method Classification (Nielsen Norman Group)

### 20 UX Research Methods

Methods are mapped across **3 dimensions**:
1. **Attitudinal vs. Behavioral**: What people say vs. what people do
2. **Qualitative vs. Quantitative**: Understanding context vs. measuring patterns
3. **Context of Use**: Natural use vs. Scripted use

**Source**: [Guide to UX Research Methods - NN/g](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/guide-ux-research-methods/)

---

## Core Methods by Category

### Discovery & Exploration
- **User Interviews**: 1-on-1 conversations about needs, pain points, context
- **Contextual Inquiry**: Observing users in their environment
- **Diary Studies**: Longitudinal self-reporting over days/weeks
- **Field Studies**: Extended observation in natural context

### Evaluation & Validation
- **Usability Testing**: Task-based observation with think-aloud
- **Heuristic Evaluation**: Expert review against usability principles
- **Cognitive Walkthrough**: Step-through user task flows
- **A/B Testing**: Quantitative comparison of design variants

### Measurement & Benchmarking
- **System Usability Scale (SUS)**: 10-item standardized questionnaire
- **Single Ease Question (SEQ)**: Post-task difficulty rating
- **Task Success Rate**: Percentage completing tasks successfully
- **Time on Task**: Efficiency measurement

### Generative & Strategic
- **Card Sorting**: Understanding mental models and IA
- **Tree Testing**: Evaluating navigation structure
- **Surveys**: Large-scale attitude and behavior measurement
- **Analytics Analysis**: Behavioral data from product usage

**Source**: [UX Research Cheat Sheet - NN/g](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/ux-research-cheat-sheet/)

## Best Practices

### Use Multiple Methods

**Principle**: Nearly all projects benefit from combining methods and insights.

**Recommended combinations**:
- Qualitative + Quantitative: Pair interviews with SUS scores
- Behavioral + Attitudinal: Combine usability testing with surveys
- Discovery + Validation: Start with interviews, validate with testing

**Why**: Triangulation increases confidence, different methods reveal different insights.

**Source**: [When to Use Which Methods - NN/g](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/which-ux-research-methods/)

---

### Survey Best Practices

**Three testing methods**:
1. **Cognitive Walkthroughs**: Test question clarity with target users
2. **Mechanical Tests**: Ensure software functions (skip logic, branching)
3. **Usability Tests**: Observe real user interaction with survey

**Critical**: Always pilot test surveys before deployment.

**Source**: [NN/g Research Methods](https://www.nngroup.com/topic/research-methods/)

---

### Authenticity in Research (2025 Trend)

**Principle**: Synthetic users are fake users generated by AI.

**Guideline**: User research needs real users. While there may be limited use cases for AI-generated personas, primary research must involve actual target users.

**Why**: AI cannot replicate the nuance, context, and unexpected insights from real users.

**Source**: [The UX Reckoning: Prepare for 2025 and Beyond - NN/g](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/ux-reset-2025/)

## 2024-2025 Trends

### AI Integration in Research Tools

**Trend**: AI features in design and research platforms (Figma, Dovetail) became more useful in 2024.

**2025 expectation**: AI will increasingly power tools to accelerate and improve work in reliable, responsible ways.

**Use cases**:
- Automated transcription and coding
- Pattern recognition in qualitative data
- Synthesis assistance (not replacement)

**Caution**: AI assists but does not replace human researcher judgment.

**Source**: [The UX Reckoning - NN/g](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/ux-reset-2025/)

---

### Combining Qualitative and Quantitative

**Best practice**: Pair qualitative methods with quantitative metrics.

**Example combinations**:
- User interviews + SUS (System Usability Scale)
- Usability testing + SEQ (Single Ease Question)
- Field studies + Analytics data

**Benefit**: Understand the "why" behind the "what" - metrics show what's happening, qualitative reveals why.

**Source**: [NN/g Research Methods](https://www.nngroup.com/topic/research-methods/)

## Research Process Workflow

### Typical UX Research Flow

**Phase 1: Planning (1-2 days)**
- Define research questions
- Select methods
- Recruit participants
- Prepare materials

**Phase 2: Data Collection (3-7 days)**
- Conduct sessions (interviews, tests)
- Observe and document
- Iterate if needed

**Phase 3: Analysis (2-5 days)**
- Synthesize findings
- Identify patterns
- Prioritize insights

**Phase 4: Communication (1-3 days)**
- Create deliverables
- Present findings
- Provide recommendations

**Total timeline**: 1-4 weeks depending on scope

## Sample Sizes

### Qualitative Methods
- **User Interviews**: 5-8 users per segment
- **Usability Testing**: 5 users (per Jakob Nielsen's research)
- **Contextual Inquiry**: 6-12 users

**Principle**: Qualitative aims for depth, not statistical significance.

### Quantitative Methods
- **Surveys**: 100+ for basic patterns, 400+ for segmentation
- **A/B Testing**: Calculate based on effect size and confidence level
- **Analytics**: Varies by traffic volume

**Principle**: Quantitative aims for statistical confidence.

## Quality Criteria

**High-quality UX research demonstrates**:
- Clear research questions
- Appropriate method selection
- Representative participants
- Systematic data collection
- Rigorous analysis
- Actionable recommendations

**Red flags**:
- Synthetic/fake users
- Leading questions
- Confirmation bias in analysis
- Cherry-picked findings

---

## When to Use UX Research

**✅ Use when**:
- Designing new features or products
- Identifying usability problems
- Understanding user needs
- Validating design decisions
- Prioritizing improvements

**❌ Skip when**:
- Decision already made (research won't influence)
- Timeline too short for proper research
- Resources insufficient for quality work

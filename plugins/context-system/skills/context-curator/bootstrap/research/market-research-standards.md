# Market Research Standards

**Purpose**: Best practices for market research and competitive analysis methodology

**Created**: 2026-02-08
**Status**: Research synthesis for `/researcher` skill implementation

---

## Overview

Market research encompasses methods to understand market dynamics, competitive landscape, customer needs, and business opportunities.

**Core principle**: Combine qualitative (why) and quantitative (how many) approaches for robust insights.

---

## Research Strategy Framework

### Key Components

1. **Objectives**: What decisions will this research inform?
2. **Methodology**: Qualitative vs. Quantitative vs. Mixed
3. **Resources**: Budget, timeline, tools required
4. **Competitors**: Who to analyze and why
5. **Evaluation criteria**: SWOT, market share, customer sentiment

**Source**: [Market Research Methods - Brandwatch](https://www.brandwatch.com/blog/market-research-methods/)

---

## Two Main Approaches

### Qualitative Methods

**Purpose**: Understand the "why" behind consumer behavior

**Methods**:
- **Focus Groups**: 6-10 participants discussing product/concept
- **In-Depth Interviews**: 1-on-1 exploration of attitudes and motivations
- **Ethnographic Observation**: Observing customer behavior in context
- **Online Communities**: Ongoing discussion forums

**When to use**: Exploratory research, understanding motivations, concept testing

---

### Quantitative Methods

**Purpose**: Measure the "how many" and identify patterns

**Methods**:
- **Surveys**: Large-scale questionnaires (n=100+)
- **Sales Data Analysis**: Historical transaction data
- **Web Analytics**: Traffic, conversions, user behavior metrics
- **Market Sizing**: TAM, SAM, SOM calculations

**When to use**: Validating hypotheses, measuring market size, tracking trends

**Source**: [Guide to Market Research - Serpstat](https://serpstat.com/blog/guide-to-market-research-methods-tools-and-best-practices/)

## Competitive Analysis Best Practices

### Structured Framework

**Six-step process**:
1. **Define scope**: What aspect of competition to analyze
2. **Identify competitors**: Direct, indirect, and emerging
3. **Gather data**: Multiple sources (primary and secondary)
4. **Analyze strengths/weaknesses**: SWOT analysis
5. **Identify opportunities/threats**: Market gaps and risks
6. **Synthesize insights**: Actionable recommendations

**Source**: [Competitive Analysis Framework - Slideworks](https://slideworks.io/resources/competitive-analysis-framework-and-template)

---

### Identifying Competitors

**Four sources**:
1. **Online searches**: Keywords for product/service category
2. **Industry resources**: Market research reports, directories, trade associations
3. **Social media & reviews**: Where customers discuss solutions
4. **Sales team insights**: Frequent competitive matchups in deals

**Beyond obvious**: Include indirect competitors and potential new entrants.

**Source**: [Competitor Analysis Guide - Kadence](https://kadence.com/en-us/knowledge/competitive-analysis/)

## Key Metrics

### Performance Metrics

**Quantitative**:
- Conversions and ROI
- Market share (revenue, units, customers)
- Customer acquisition cost (CAC)
- Customer lifetime value (LTV)

**Qualitative**:
- Customer sentiment (NPS, reviews, social listening)
- Brand perception
- Product positioning effectiveness

---

### Competitor SEO Metrics

**Key indicators**:
- **Visibility**: Ranking positions for target keywords
- **Organic traffic**: Estimated search traffic volume
- **Referring domains**: Backlink profile strength
- **Content gaps**: Topics competitors rank for but you don't

**Tools**: Semrush, Ahrefs, Serpstat

**Source**: [Market Research Methods - Serpstat](https://serpstat.com/blog/guide-to-market-research-methods-tools-and-best-practices/)

## Data Collection Sources

### Primary Research (Original Data)

- Customer surveys and interviews
- Focus groups
- Mystery shopping
- Field observations
- Sales team feedback

---

### Secondary Research (Existing Data)

**Public sources**:
- Government statistics (Census, BLS, industry reports)
- Industry associations and trade groups
- Academic research and journals
- Company financial filings (10-K, investor reports)

**Paid sources**:
- Market research reports (Gartner, Forrester, IBISWorld)
- Analyst briefings
- Subscription databases

**Digital sources**:
- Competitor websites and blogs
- Social media profiles
- Review sites (G2, Capterra, Trustpilot)
- News and press releases

## Competitive Benchmarking Best Practices

### Framework Requirements

**Effective benchmarking includes**:
- **Industry-specific metrics**: Relevant KPIs for your sector
- **Quantitative + Qualitative data**: Financial KPIs + customer sentiment
- **Data accuracy**: Verified sources, consistent measurement
- **Cross-functional involvement**: Input from sales, marketing, product, finance

**Avoid**:
- Vanity metrics without business impact
- Cherry-picked data that confirms bias
- Outdated information

**Source**: [Competitive Benchmarking Process - Valona Intelligence](https://valonaintelligence.com/resources/blog/competitive-benchmarking-for-insights-in-market-research-process)

## Market Sizing Framework

### Three-Tier Approach

1. **TAM (Total Addressable Market)**: Total demand if you had 100% market share
2. **SAM (Serviceable Available Market)**: Portion of TAM you can realistically reach
3. **SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market)**: Portion you can realistically capture

**Methods**:
- **Top-down**: Start with industry data, narrow to segment
- **Bottom-up**: Build from unit economics and customer counts
- **Value theory**: Estimate based on value delivered to customers

## Timeline

**Typical market research project**:
- **Secondary research**: 1-2 weeks
- **Primary research** (surveys): 2-3 weeks
- **Primary research** (interviews): 2-4 weeks
- **Competitive analysis**: 1-2 weeks
- **Synthesis and reporting**: 1 week

**Total**: 1-4 weeks depending on scope and methods

---

## Quality Criteria

**High-quality market research demonstrates**:
- Clear business objectives
- Appropriate method selection
- Representative sample (if quantitative)
- Multiple data sources (triangulation)
- Objective analysis (acknowledges limitations)
- Actionable recommendations

**Red flags**:
- Single source dependence
- Outdated data (>2 years old in fast-moving markets)
- Biased sampling
- Unsupported conclusions

---

## When to Use Market Research

**✅ Use when**:
- Entering new markets
- Launching new products
- Understanding competitive threats
- Identifying growth opportunities
- Strategic planning and positioning

**❌ Skip when**:
- Market too niche for available data
- Decision already made
- Pivot required regardless of data
